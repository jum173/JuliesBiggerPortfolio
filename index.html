Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

All 3 of these things are involved in creating. Rhetoric is the conversation around the topic of what you're making in today's world and needs to be considered so that your creation isn't perceived as insensitive or lacking background information. Technology in creating plays a part in accessibility. Technology needs to be accessible to anyone of impairments or dissabilites. Ethics goes hand in hand with both of these things as the things you create must be of good moral standing, for example, creating an ad for a product that may make you money but is harmful to others needs to have all side effects and consequences of using on the advertisement for it to be ethical.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

Design principles I used were understanding what some colors mean and what emotions they evoke. I also utilized space and whiteness in keeping certain designs clean while others more jampacked to maximize fun and information, such as in the NJ infographic.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Working with others in a workshop was very helpful to my design progression. My peers were able to tell me how they interpretted by designs and I was able to decide if their interpretation matched my intention for the design. Additionally, seeing their designs and methodologies in creating through the softwares we were learning gave me my own inspiration and ideas for future designs.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

In this class, I was able to practice revision. When my peers or professor give me feedback, or even when I spot something I can enhance or make better, I go back and revise my work. I do so with a trial-and-error approach where I duplicate designs and tweak things, seeing if they'll look better, keeping the safety of my original design in case it does not.

5. Learn to present your work.

I think I was always good at presenting and showing my enthusiasm and curiosity. Presenting to my small group and also to the entire class made me even more comfortable with taking critism in real time and asking follow up questions for feedback.
